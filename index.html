About

I'm a sophomore at Los Altos High School in Los Altos, CA. I tend to notice when something isn't working and then keep going until I figure out why. That's how CivicPulse started. I kept seeing potholes and broken streetlights that weren't getting fixed, and realized there was no easy way for residents to tell the city what needed attention. So I built one.

Outside of software, I mentor about 60 students a year in Math Kangaroo prep, compete on VEX and FRC robotics teams, and run a STEM Research Club I started at school to connect students with research at nearby universities.

Projects

CivicPulse Town Crier article ↗

CivicPulse is a platform that lets Los Altos residents report local infrastructure issues, from potholes to broken streetlights, upvote what matters most to them, and track whether anything gets done about it. Residents can drop a pin, upload a photo, and submit a report in under a minute. The app includes a live map of all active reports, community upvoting so the most urgent issues rise to the top, push notifications, and an admin dashboard for city staff. It was covered by the Los Altos Town Crier and is in active use with LAMVCF and the Rotary Club. The iOS app is live on the App Store, with Android submitted for review.

React, TypeScript, Firebase, Leaflet.js, Tailwind CSS, iOS, Android

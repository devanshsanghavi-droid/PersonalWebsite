About

Systems thinking,
shipped as software.

I'm a sophomore at Los Altos High School, but I don't build school projects — I build real software that real people use.

Most of what I make starts the same way: I notice a system failing — a city that can't track its own potholes, a robot that can't navigate on its own — and I work backwards from the failure mode until I've fixed it. That instinct runs through everything, from diagnosing autonomous navigation drift on our VEX robot to designing a civic reporting app the city actually adopted.

When I'm not shipping, I'm competing in math, mentoring students through it, and machining parts for our FRC team. I care about the work being good, not about how it looks on a transcript.

FEATURED · LIVE

CivicPulse

A civic engagement platform that lets Los Altos residents report local infrastructure issues — potholes, broken streetlights, graffiti — upvote community priorities, and track resolution end-to-end. Real-time clustered maps, GPS-tagged photo uploads, push notifications, and an admin dashboard for city officials. Published in the Town Crier; in active use with LAMVCF and the Rotary Club. iOS and Android apps submitted to the app stores.
